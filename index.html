Blockchain

A blockchain is a continuously growing list of records, called blocks, which are linked and secured using cryptography.

Each block typically contains a hash pointer as a link to a previous block, a timestamp and transaction data. By design, blockchains are inherently resistant to modification of the data. It is open, a distributed ledger that can record transactions between two parties efficiently and in a verifiable and in a permanent way. A blockchain is typically managed by a peer-to-peer network.
